Dishes offered by the restaurant

Arroz Carreteiro

Representing the southern region of the country, we have arroz carreteiro, typical of Rio Grande do Sul. The dish came about when the carreteiros (cargo carriers) who traveled in carts pulled by oxen pulled by oxen cooked a mixture of rice and sun-dried meat stew in an iron pot. meat stew. As it was a practical recipe, it was easily prepared by a lone traveler. The dish was incorporated into Brazilian cuisine and today it is quite common to find and in all regions of the country.

Acarajé

Acarajé is a delicious delicacy prepared with a handmade bean cake, seasoned with onion and salt, fried in palm oil and then filled with vatapá (coconut milk, cashew nuts, peanuts and shrimp), vinaigrette and dried shrimp. Incidentally, if someone asks you if you want your acarajé cold or hot, they are not referring to the temperature at which it is served. the temperature at which it is served, but rather the amount of chili pepper you add!

Bobó de camarão

Bobó is well known throughout Brazil, especially in the Northeast. prawns sautéed in coconut milk, palm oil and green spices. Its origins are Afro-Brazilian and the dish may also have had an indigenous influence.

Feijoada

The traditional dish consists of black beans, matured meats (carne-seca, carne de sol, etc.) and pork parts (ears, tongue, tail, loin and ribs, as well as sausages), and served with white rice, cabbage, farofa, pork rinds and oranges. served with white rice, cabbage, farofa, pork rinds and oranges. Our feijoada is a complete meal and very popular among Brazilians, especially at gatherings of friends and family on weekends, holidays and commemorative dates.

Virado á Paulista

During the expeditions of the 17th century, in colonial Brazil, foods such as corn flour, beans, bacon and dried meat were packed together and rattled along the way, getting turned over. Thus, virado à paulista was born Over the years, the recipe gained new ingredients, such as pork, fried egg, rice, sausage, cabbage, pork rinds and plantains.
